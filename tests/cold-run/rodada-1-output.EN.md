## 1. Findings — detailed (by severity)

**[F-01] · height 1 · ELIMINATORY · CONFIRMED — The proposal is on the 2022 form, and the 2026 form is a different one**

- Draft excerpt: "FICC – 2022 / ANEXO VI – MODELO DE PROPOSTA … 1.4. Categoria: Música Brasileira Autoral … 4. Plano de Contrapartida pelo Benefício"
- Anchor in the call: Anexo II, item 2.4 — "Project Proposal, in the form of Anexo VI". The 2026 Anexo VI has different fields: 1.3 is "Modalidade/categoria:", there is no separate "Categoria" field; there is a new field 2.9 "Faixa Etária:" (age range); section 3 is now called "Plano de Acesso e Acessibilidade" (Access and Accessibility Plan); section 4 is "Cronograma de desenvolvimento do projeto" (project development schedule) in the "Mês 01 - Etapa" format; and there is no "Contrapartida pelo Benefício" (counterpart-for-benefit) section at all. And the call, 21.8: "Failure to observe any item of this call will imply the rejection of the cultural project."
- Why it matters: submitting on the old template means mandatory fields missing (Faixa Etária), sections that no longer exist taking up space, and naming that signals to the evaluator that the project was not written for this call.
- What to do: transpose the content, field by field, into the 2026 Anexo VI (download the official PDF). This is the skeleton of everything else in this review.

**[F-03] · heights 1 and 3 · ELIMINATORY · CONFIRMED — Venues without letters of consent (and the list mixes venues that are exempt with venues that require one)**

- Draft excerpt: "Centro Cultural Casarão … (this cultural venue is managed by an artists' collective…)", "Sala dos Toninhos … managed by a collective", "Sala de Cinema da Casa do Lago at Unicamp", "Tucum", "Pró-música and/or Chorus", "Rabeca Cultural or Pavão Cultural"
- Anchor in the call: Anexo II, 2.7 — "Indication of the venues where the project's actions will take place, with a letter of consent and/or commitment of interest from the institution responsible for managing the space, identifying the person who signs on behalf of the institution, where applicable, the letter being waived in the case of venues managed by the Municipal Secretariat of Culture and Tourism".
- Why it matters: it is a mandatory registration document. Your own draft says Casarão and Sala dos Toninhos are managed by collectives — that is, by your own description, they need letters. Casa do Lago (Unicamp), Tucum, Chorus, Rabeca and Pavão certainly need them. And "Pró-música and/or Chorus", "Rabeca or Pavão" — an indication with "or" neither defines the venue nor makes a letter feasible. The official help-desk's interpretation (13–21 Jul 26) confirms: every space outside the Secretariat's management requires a letter, with no official template, signed by the direct manager; for Secretariat venues, attach a simple document naming the venue and its management. Which venues on your list are in fact managed by the Secretariat today (and not by collectives or by Education/Unicamp) — confirm with the mediators.
- What to do: with 9 days left, this is race-against-the-clock #1. Close the definitive venue list (no "or"), separate Secretariat-managed venues from the rest, and start requesting the letters for the rest TODAY. The help desk (16 Jul 26) admits including a space temporarily unable to issue a letter, with a justification in the project — use only as a last resort.

**[F-04] · heights 1 and 3 · LOSES POINTS · CONFIRMED — The "Contrapartida pelo Benefício" section no longer exists**

**[F-05] · heights 1 and 3 · LOSES POINTS · CONFIRMED — The 2022 "Social Counterpart" does not answer the 2026 "Access and Accessibility Plan" criterion**

- Draft excerpt: "3. Plano de Contrapartida Social … All performances of the show Espiral em Mim will be free…"
- Anchor in the call: item 7.3 — "The Access and Accessibility Plan must also include actions that facilitate free access for the elderly and people with disabilities or reduced mobility and/or communicational accessibility measures" (7.3.1.2 lists Libras [Brazilian sign language], Braille, audio description, subtitles, plain language); item 7.5 — in the case of "unrestricted free admission", the agent must "justify the project's inherent benefits for the population at large"; and the 12.1 grid scores the plan 0 to 5: "the coherence of the actions aimed at democratizing access to the project's results will be evaluated… its impact and artistic-cultural benefits for the community". Careful: what you write there becomes an execution obligation (17.1.1).
- Why it matters: that is 5 of the 30 points, in a call where a total score ≤15 disqualifies (12.5 — "Projects scoring a total equal to or lower than 15 (fifteen) will be disqualified."). Free admission alone, with no concrete accessibility measure, falls short of what the criterion describes.
- What to do: keep the free admission (justifying it in the terms of 7.5), pick at least one measure from the 7.6 menu (the open rehearsal from your "Option 2" fits 7.6.3; the Maria Monteiro performance speaks to 7.6.2) and add accessibility measures from 7.3 that you can actually deliver — without promising what the budget cannot hold.

**[F-06] · heights 1 and 3 · LOSES POINTS · CONFIRMED — Without the Ficha Técnica (Anexo V) and without Anexo IV, you give up as many as 5 inducer points your team probably already earns**

- Draft excerpt: the proposal lists the team in prose ("Regiane Bataglini on vocals, Fernando Baeta conducting… Cris Monteiro… Júlia Toledo… Marissa Garcino… Wanessa Crispim…"), but there is no Ficha Técnica (team roster) nor inducer declaration.
- Anchor in the call: Anexo II, 2.2 and 2.3 (Anexos IV and V mandatory at registration); 12.7.1 — "To obtain the inclusion inducers, the submitted team roster must consist of at least 5 members, these being responsible for the 5 highest-paid roles in the project."; 12.9.2 — "female-gender persons: 40% of the participants on the team roster, including the proposing cultural agent, must self-declare as female"; 12.9.5 — territorial deconcentration: "the proposing cultural agent's proof of residence must be from a neighborhood not located in the Barão Geraldo or Centro APG (Planning and Management Area)."
- Why it matters: one point per inducer met, up to 5 — and the cut-off score is harsh. Your band is mostly women; the design of the team roster (who holds the 5 best-paid roles in the budget) decides whether the 40% inducer closes or not. Mind the help desk's interpretive detail (16–20 Jul 26): to score, the proposer must belong to the inducer's group — the team alone is not enough. Who the proposer will be (you? Regiane?) changes which inducers are reachable; and the deconcentration one depends only on the proposer's address.
- What to do: build the Ficha Técnica together with the budget sheet (the two lock each other through 12.7.1), check which inducers the team's real composition meets, and collect Anexo IV signatures only from those who fit the profile — a false declaration incurs articles 171 and 299 of the Penal Code (warning on the form itself).

**[F-07] · height 1 · ELIMINATORY · CONFIRMED — Schedule and dates are from 2023 and clash with the 2026/27 execution window**

- Draft excerpt: "Execution period: May to December /2023 … May/2023: Rehearsals… June/2023: Publicity and opening show…" (and the typos "Outubro/2223").
